Role: UX Architecture, Visual Design and Branding, Code

Scribe is a web-based sheet music notation program.

When I began work on Scribe, I was preparing to go to grad school for music composition. As a composer, I was frustrated by most modern sheet music notation software. The most popular programs are chiefly targeted at professional copyists and publishers, with interfaces optimized for fine-grained control over layout and typography. While this is great for publishers, it results in a subpar experience for composers.

I built Scribe as a proof-of-concept for an interface more suited to composers and songwriters, letting them focusing on the content of their music rather than its presentation.

Process

Scribe began as a series of rough HTML prototypes experimenting with different forms of inputting notes via multitouch on the iPad. I jumped straight into code, sketching in CSS rather than paper. I was trying to see what could come out of a conscious radical departure from current sheet music software interface conventions.

As a composer myself, I was initially the only user of the app. As I became convinced of its viability, I began conducting user research to see where my assumptions about other people's compositional processes were wrong. I observed composers using existing software, getting a good sense of others' workflows and notational needs.

As a result, I began to focus on creating an experience that would work for both iPad and desktop, rather than chiefly the iPad.

Result

A live prototype of Scribe is available at http://scribeapp.herokuapp.com.
