Role: UX Architecture, Visual Design, Front-End Code

Buying college textbooks is frustrating. The school bookstore is convenient but expensive; online comparison shopping online is cheap, but time-consuming.

In college, two of my friends and I built a comparison shopping engine that let students look up their books by entering what courses they were taking. Although we all had a hand in all parts of the business, I was roughly in charge of the design of the product and the front-end codebase.

Process

The initial version of GetchaBooks was built over a period of 2-3 weeks. My co-founder Mike built the back-end while my other co-founder Ricky and I paired on the design and code for the front-end.

After launching at two schools as a pilot program, we were able to interview users and perform in-person user testing to gather feedback about what worked and what didn't. Based on the set of personas that came out of that, we began to fine-tune and redesign the site so that it could better suit those different needs, including a visual redesign and a complete overhaul of the results page.

The new version of the product launched at hundreds of schools, rather than the two we initially supported. A custom-built analytics system I built let us use our larger userbase to fine-tune the site with A/B testing and other mechanisms of data-driven decision making over the next couple of years.

Results

At the peak of our success, we were used by students at over a thousand universities across America. At Tufts University (our "home base") over 60% of the undergraduate student body used GetchaBooks to buy their textbooks. We were featured in publications such as Inc. Magazine and the NY Times.

While GetchaBooks is no longer live, the entire application has been open-sourced on GitHub.
