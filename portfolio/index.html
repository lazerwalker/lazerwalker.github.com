Hi. I'm Mike.

I'm a designer and a developer. I mostly work on games, mobile apps, and rich web apps.

As a designer, I...

Favor working software. Putting a flawed prototype in the hands of real users reveals truths that practices like interviewing or low-fidelity testing often aren't equipped to expose. I believe that delivering functioning software as early in the design process as possible and rapidly iterating is the best way to test your hypotheses.

Sketch directly in code. Creating a tight feedback loop for working software is easiest when you're working natively in your medium. Thanks to my engineering background, I'm comfortable jumping straight into code to try out new ideas, whether that means writing HTML/CSS, native Objective-C, or even server-side code.

Embrace metrics, but only to a point. I've rolled my own homegrown analytics systems. I once increased installs 40% on a Facebook game with 4 million DAU in a single A/B test. But data-driven design that can only take you so far; while knowing how and what to A/B test can make your product better, knowing when to rely on intuition instead of numbers is just as important.

Seek out active collaboration. Talking about "my design" versus "your design" just gets in the way of building the best possible product. I believe that nothing produces better work than tight-knit, ego-less collaboration. I love to pair, both with designers and developers.
